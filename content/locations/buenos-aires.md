Buenos Aires is one of those cities that takes you completely by surprise. I arrived expecting a stopover and ended up staying six weeks. There is something about the pace of the place — late dinners, afternoon coffee that stretches into evening, Sunday markets that don't get going until noon — that makes the rest of the world feel slightly frantic by comparison. The city is enormous (population 3 million in the city proper, 15 million in Greater BA) and deeply neighbourhoood-defined. Palermo is not San Telmo is not La Boca. Getting comfortable in one corner takes a week; understanding the whole city takes much longer. I'd recommend staying at least two weeks if you can.
