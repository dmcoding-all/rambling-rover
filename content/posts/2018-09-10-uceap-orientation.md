The study abroad program I am going to Edinburgh through is the
University of California Education Abroad Program. They hosted an
orientation for all students going to Scottish Universities the first
two days I was there (the 6th and 7th). This was a great opportunity for
me to meet people who would also be here and make quick friends. They
took us out and showed us around the city.
At night, some people from orientation (a mixed group of students
attending the University of Edinburgh and St Andrews (fun fact, St
Andrew’s was established before the invention of the period, so they are
adamant that there is no period after “St”) went and grabbed a meal the
first night.
Finally, I learned that Scotland’s national animal is a Unicorn. Why?
You might ask? Because a Unicorn is said to be the only animal to be
able to defeat a lion... the national animal of
England.
