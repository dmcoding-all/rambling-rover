## The Lemon Squeezer

Often these places are just another trick the trail throws at you.
But every once and a while, the obstacle becomes so well known, it gets
a name. One such place is The Lemon Squeezer. First, The Lemon Squeezer
takes hikers through a tight opening between two large rocks.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120923/IMG_1044-scaled.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120923/IMG_1044-scaled.jpg)

Then, one must walk between another two large rocks, and as the hiker
moves forward through this opening, it gets more and more narrow (i.e.,
it squeezes you).

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094557/IMG_1677.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094557/IMG_1677.jpg)

Some hikers (like myself) may opt to take off their backpacks to
wriggle through.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094553/IMG_1678.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094553/IMG_1678.jpg)

Others, like my friend Crushing It, are more creative and just climb
atop.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120945/IMG_1048-scaled-e1687889753954.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120945/IMG_1048-scaled-e1687889753954.jpg)

Even more fun in my opinion is that after the squeeze, one must
immediately scale a vertical rock face. In my opinion, this climb in
itself is not dangerous or necessarily hard, adding a giant backpacking
pack on your back makes it much more tricky.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094545/IMG_6788-scaled.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05094545/IMG_6788-scaled.jpg)

## Deli-Blazing

Another great thing about New York (albeit not specific to only New
York, because it begins a bit in New Jersey) is that hikers on the AT
get to Deli-blaze. What is deli-blazing? you ask. Well, as I you may
understand after reading [my post about
various trail terms](https://theramblingrover.com/trail-terms-to-know/), "blazing" is a generic term used to indicate
the way in which hikers follow the path. Deli-blazing means that there
are deli's (actually, its food-places of all sorts) close enough to the
trail to be walk-able. They are so common along the trail in fact that
hikers are known to bounce from one to the other, almost hitting a deli
at least once a day. 

Some examples of food one can deli-blaze on the trail that is not an
actual deli includes a[
Malaysian Food Truck](https://www.facebook.com/p/DC-Malaysian-Food-100077743098808/) and a [creamery](https://bellvalefarms.com/appalachian-trail-community/#)
(where I went to on one particularly rainy day and it had amazing Ice
Cream).

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120911/IMG_1021-scaled-e1687889650887.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27120911/IMG_1021-scaled-e1687889650887.jpg)

Then there are places like [The Appalachian Market](https://www.appalachianmarketny.com/),
which is literally right on the trail, that have a deli inside, plus
they sell food and gear for resupply!

## The Trailside Zoo

[The
Trailside Zoo](https://parks.ny.gov/environment/nature-centers/20/details.aspx) was something I did not expect and I only heard about
it a few days before coming upon it myself. It is as it sounds: a zoo
right along the trail. but what was/is especially cool about it is that
it is free! Donation only! and thanks to it, I can now say I have
officially seen not one but two bears on the trail!

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121247/IMG_1110-scaled-e1687889607431.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121247/IMG_1110-scaled-e1687889607431.jpg)

The Trailside Zoo is right after Bear Mountain. After coming down
from the mountain, I met up with my friend Gyatso, and even though it
was pouring rain, we decided it was worth it to check out the zoo.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121230/IMG_1108-scaled-e1687889579820.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121230/IMG_1108-scaled-e1687889579820.jpg)

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05113856/IMG_1111-scaled-e1688578779882.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/05113856/IMG_1111-scaled-e1688578779882.jpg)

We wandered through, and even though most animals were hidden away
due to the rain, the bears were out, they had an indoor reptile house
unaffected by the downpour, and they had a geology of the region
exhibit! So I still enjoyed the zoo :)

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121302/IMG_1123-scaled.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27121302/IMG_1123-scaled.jpg)
