Even though we had a 5am flight the following money to Dublin
(meaning we wake up at 3am), when we found out that a ceilidh was
happening Thursday night, every one of the lemons that could make it
decided to go.![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1275.jpg)![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1182-1.jpg)

That's because this was our very very last chance to go to a ceilidh,
and the best part was that we went together as one big group (of lemons)
whereas previously we had only managed to go in little sub-groups. Also
some of us, like Eleanor and Alessandra, had not been to a ceilidh
before.

![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1235.jpg)

The greatest part was that, since we have now been to so many
ceilidhs. We were the group that others were looking to when they were
trying to figure out how to do the dance. That probably was also due to
the fact that we had all three of the guys in the whole room who were
wearing kilts--which automatically add authenticity to their dance
moves. We had such a blast. Even when others were not dancing, Adam,
Jack or Flo would grab one of us (or each other) and dance,

it was a great chance for all of us to sort of wrap up the
semester

![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/994b2-img_1199-small.jpeg)

![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/6b36d-img_1197-medium.jpeg)

for example, I met Jack at my very first ceilidh. I didn't know it
then, but we would keep in contact and he would bring in Claire and
George and the three of them would bring us lots of joy. So jack and I
agreed that we had to end the semester how we started it, and so for the
last two dances... the one where you spin and spin and spin, and the one
where you sprint across the room, we were partners ![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1215.jpg)

We even did my favorite Ceilidh dance, the one where you pair up and
get in groups of 8, and then four of you join together and the guys pick
you up and spin you... often so fast that the girls get lifted off their
feet. When I did that dance, we had Adam, Flo, Jack, and George as our
guys, so we (the girls) FLEW (see Claire's
legs *flying*)![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1278.jpg)

even after the official ceilidh ended, we kept dancing and then we
went outside, messing around, taking photos, and just enjoying all being
together![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1216.jpg)![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/img_1276.jpg)

I couldn't have asked for a better way to tie bow on this
semester

**EVERYONE IF YOU ARE EVER IN SCOTLAND GO TO A
CEILIDH**

granted while I wouldn't say we regretted it, when we woke up the
