I moved into my dorm room (photos to come) on
the 8th. I met my roommate, another american study abroad student from
Connecticut who goes to school at American University. The dorm RA's
organize lots of fun events for freshmen to get to know each other, and
I have been going to as many as I can. The dorms I live in are the main
location for international students. This is great because I am meeting
people from all over, and since everyone is new, and they are all mostly
freshmen, they are DYING to make friends. This means I can literally
walk up to almost anyone and strike up a conversation and by the end of
it we are exchanging information. The hard part is remembering all the
names and faces.

As expected, since spending time is allowed here, freshmen events are a lot
different than in the states. For example, on the first night, about 300
of us went to a local pub, the Pear Tree (poor pub amiright?). We
grabbed a meal and got to sit and get to know each other. The photos
above are two I took at the venue that I found pretty entertaining. I love
the kilt and if you are wondering, yes that is the millennium falcon
flying above the Edinburgh Castle, and yes the guy on the left
definitely thought I was taking a photo of him.
