I just wanted to take a moment to tell all of you how lucky I have been
with the group of people who have become my friends while I've been
here. I have always had this vision of studying abroad and becoming best
friends with those who I'm studying abroad with. Other programs often
house study abroad students together, that way they have each other as
support in the new country, and often this leads to close friendships
where you end up traveling together and experiencing the culture of the
host country together.
But my host program didn't do that.
When we arrived we had 48 hours where I met the other students in my
program, and after that we all moved into our accommodations which were
spread throughout the city. I'll be honest, I was a bit disheartened. I
had gotten along really well with them and I knew that with everyone
living separate, I would most likely not experience that continued
closeness with them that I had always imagined I would. I was at the
same time freaking out because I was moving into a double, and for those
who know, I did not get along very well with my roommate freshmen year
at UC berkeley.
But then I moved in, and my roommate and I got along. She is also an
American study abroad student. I believe choosing to have a roommate was
the best choice I could have made, because it gave me an instant friend.
Her and I went to a bunch of dorm hosted events and met all the
freshmen. At the beginning we both felt a little as if the other
freshmen did not really want to become close friends with us because it
was not a good investment of their time since we would just be leaving
at the end of the semester. But then we went on the group led hike up
Arthurs seat where we met Claire. Claire is a freshmen who is also from
the states. After that we started meeting other exchange students, and
after a night at a ceilidh, we became friends with two guys who were our
dance partners. It has been a weird, random assortment of people, but
like me, my roommate was determined to have a friend group. And so we
kept inviting everyone we met to join us when we went out.
Long story short, last night we had our first (apparently not our last)
Monday Mask night. And not only did all the girls in our friend group
join, but the guys came too (although I'm not sure they really realized
what they were getting themselves into). Tonight has really just made me
grateful. I've always desired to have a big group of friends who are
down to do adventurous, fun, silly things together. At Cal I have met
some amazing people, but it took me a couple years before I really felt
like I found my place there. God has honestly blessed me by making me
feel at home so soon in a new country. Since I am only here for really 3
months, it doesn't really give me a lot of time to make friends slowly.
These are people who I have now booked flight trips with. People who
agree to wake up at 7 am with me to go hiking, or go swimming. People
who you (my followers) will probably be seeing a lot of in future posts
:)
