This was originally posted on The Trek, [here](https://thetrek.co/what-i-have-packed-for-the-appalachian-trail/). 

Being that I had never thru-hiked before, I did not have a ton of
ultralight gear prior to the AT. As I began purchasing equipment, I kept
in mind that I wanted to strike a balance between sticking with items I
already had if they would do the job an, if necessary, purchasing new
things that fit the bill but didn't break the bank. That being said...
my bank still hurt when all was said and done...

I break down my pack by [The Big
4](#big-4) (Pack + Shelter + Sleep system), [Clothing + Footwear](#clothing+footwear), [Electronics](#electronics), [Kitchen](#kitchen), and [other](#other). 

You can find these broken out by weight at my[ LighterPack link](https://lighterpack.com/r/xn8zi8).

## Big 4 (Backpack + Shelter +
Sleep system)

- **Backpack**: [Osprey M/L Eja 58L](https://www.osprey.com/us/en/)

I chose this backpack because I needed more volume than some of the
other ultralight backpacks on the market, and this backpack has great
reviews and is decently affordable.

- In the process of finally landing on this backpack, I went through
the Gossamer Gear Mariposa, [Osprey Aura LT 65](https://www.osprey.com/us/en/product/aura-ag-lt-65-AURA65LT_477.html), and the
size XS/S Osprey Eja 58

- **Shelter**:

[TarpTent Double Rainbow](https://www.tarptent.com/product/double-rainbow/). I
chose this because I wanted a lightweight tent with good reviews that
could be both free-standing. TarpTent is well known for their quality,
and customer service, and the Rainbow is know for being large. I chose
the Double Rainbow because I hope in the future to use this tent to
backpack with others.

- Footprint: Polycryo. I bough this from [**Duck Brand Window Shrink
Film** from Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B000NHY1P0?psc=1&ref=ppx_yo2ov_dt_b_product_details)

- Stakes: Easton Stakes

- **Sleeping Quilt/Bag**: 

My plan is to carry a [Hammock Gear Premium Burrow 30](https://hammockgear.com/premium-burrow-custom/) the first
half of my hike while I am hiking in the summer heat. Half way through
I'll meet up with my mom on the trail who will bring me my warmer [Sea
to Summit Women's Flame 15](https://seatosummit.com/products/flame-womens-ultralight-sleeping-bag?variant=19509665726567) sleeping bag.

- **Sleeping Pad**: 

[Therm-a-Rest NeoAir® XLite™ NXT
Sleeping Pad](https://www.thermarest.com/sleeping-pads/fast-and-light/neoair-xlite-nxt-sleeping-pad/neoair-xlite-nxt.html) because my [Sea
to Summit Women's Comfort Light Insulated Air](https://seatosummit.com/products/comfort-light-insulated-womens-sleeping-pad) popped while I was in
Joshua Tree :(

- **Sleeping Pillow**: 

[Sea to Summit Aeros Premium
Pillow](https://www.rei.com/product/151400/sea-to-summit-aeros-premium-pillow). Not technically a part of the big 4 but a part of my sleep
system and technically not even a necessary piece of gear (many
backpackers will just stuff a bag full of clothes to use as a pillow)
but considering all I am going to have to deal with as I backpack the
AT, I decided that adding this provided a little bit of extra comfort
may make a big difference and would be worth the size and weight.
