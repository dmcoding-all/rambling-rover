For those who do not know, Isle of Skye is an island on the
northwestern part of Scotland, voted as the 4th prettiest
island in the world by National Geographic. So of course my roommate and
I, plus two of our new friends, all signed up for the International and
Exchange Student Trip to the **[Isle of Skye](https://www.isleofskye.com/)**.

## Starting off the Trip

We hopped on a bus Saturday morning and headed there (it is a 5 hr
non-stop drive from Edinburgh to the Isle of Skye). However, along the
way we made multiple stops at places such as **[Loch Lomond](https://www.visitscotland.com/see-do/landscapes-nature/national-parks-gardens/loch-lomond-trossachs/)**, **[Loch Tulla](https://www.google.co.uk/search?q=loch+tulla&rlz=1C1CHBF_enUS758US759&source=lnms&tbm=isch&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwj0pqTT-fjdAhWFOsAKHbcfBTgQ_AUIDygC&biw=1368&bih=771)**, **[Glencoe](https://www.visitscotland.com/destinations-maps/glencoe/) **(some of these are
similar to my [Scottish
Highland Bus Tour](https://theramblingrover.com/my-first-rugby-match-and-highland-bus-tour/)), **[Ben Nevis](https://www.visitscotland.com/see-do/iconic-scotland/ben-nevis/)** (the tallest
mountain/Monroe in Scotland), **[Fort William](https://www.visitscotland.com/destinations-maps/fort-william/)** (where we ate
lunch among the ruins), and the **[Eilean Donan Castle](http://www.eileandonancastle.com/)** which is the most
photographed castle in all of Scotland and is featured in movies such as
*Made of Honor *(with [Patrick Dempsey](https://www.biography.com/people/patrick-dempsey-201303) aka [McDreamy](https://www.women.com/connieliou/lists/10-reasons-i-will-always-be-in-love-with-mcdreamy)), *the New Avengers,
*and *Highlander. *We drove across the Skye bridge around 6
pm and settled into our hostel before going out to explore the island.
That night we listened to live music and hung out with other
international and exchange students.

## Making the most of a dreich time

As you can see from the photos, the weather was beautiful all day
Saturday. Sunday, however, was a much different story. Let's just say
Sunday was the first day I can honestly say I experienced Scottish
weather. It was dreich (recall [The
Cobbler)](https://theramblingrover.com/climbing-the-cobbler/).

![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/07a4b-b7cde562-db19-493d-93ec-6cafbb97559d_1_105_c.jpeg)

It was rainy, cold, and windy. Despite this, we tried to make the
best of it. We first went to the **[Sligachan Bridge](https://natpacker.com/destination/sligachan-bridge/)** which takes
you over the River Sligachan. Legend has it that if you hold your face
in the water for 7 seconds, you will be blessed with eternal beauty and
youth. So of course we did it. 

![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/ec41a-4a5b3ad5-793f-4e2e-9e17-d7adc7280939_1_105_c.jpeg?w=1024&h=498)

Next, we hiked to the **[Old Man of Storr](https://www.isleofskye.com/skye-guide/top-ten-skye-walks/old-man-of-storr) **which turns
out to be these really cool sharp rock formations which jut out of this
hill into the sky. Legend has it that there was a giant who lived in the
area and when he eventually died and was buried, he was too big, and so
his fingers (note this is the PG version of the story) were left
sticking out of the ground. Unfortunately, it was far to windy and rainy
for us to make it all the way up to the Old Man himself, and even if we
had the rocks were shrouded in fog so it would not have been worth it,
but we still had fun taking more photos. After this point, everyone on
the bus was sufficiently soaked to the skin, and it took great energy
for us to get out of the bus to see the next two viewpoints: **[Mealt Falls and Loch Mealt](https://www.world-of-waterfalls.com/europe-mealt-falls.html)**,
and **[Lealt Gorge and Lealt Falls](https://www.tripadvisor.co.uk/Attraction_Review-g551883-d12363598-Reviews-Lealt_Falls-Portree_Isle_of_Skye_The_Hebrides_Scotland.html)**
(Most of my photos from this time have been lost so please click on the
links to enjoy the true beauty of these locations). Finally, we stopped
in **[Portree](https://www.isleofskye.com/portree)**—the largest town in of the Isle of
Skye—for lunch (so basically anything warm we could find) and respite
from the weather before heading back to Edinburgh.
