Delaware Water Gap is a small town in Northern Pennsylvania, and I
believed that my time there deserved a post unto its own.

You see, when the fires in Canada got really bad, and the smoke was
streaming down over northeastern US, it was here, in Delaware Water Gap
(DWG) that I sheltered from the terrible air quality. It is important
for me to note that the reason I was able to do so, was due to the
kindness and generosity of the[ Church of the Mountain
Hiker Hostel](https://churchofthemountain.org/hikers/) (fun fact, this hiker hostel is actually noted to be
the FIRST hiker hostel on the trail!).

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104046/IMG_0791-scaled-e1687884067641-700x933.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104046/IMG_0791-scaled-e1687884067641.jpg)

In the basement of this church, they have built bunk beds and a
simple shower. They also have a living room of sorts which hikers can
lounge in on couches.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104016/image000000-4-700x527.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104016/image000000-4.jpg)

On Thursday evenings, members of the church/community gather at the
church for a hiker potluck, where the church members bring food, and
they feed us hikers--their only requirement being that we spread out and
sit with the church members in order to talk and get to know them. While
donations are accepted, the church provides all of this for free. 

*I would like to add that, while I do have an option here on my
website for you [to donate and help me afford
hiking the AT](https://theramblingrover.com/donations/donation-form/), if any of you are thinking of donating, please also
consider [donating to this church](https://churchofthemountain.org/donate-to-us/). It
provides so much to hikers asking nothing in return (when so many other
hostels on the trail nickel and dime us hikers for everything), and
these are the types of facilities that I want to see continue on the
trail.*

I ended up staying almost 3 full days in DWG, hiding out from the
smoke.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104102/IMG_0809-scaled-e1687884162355-700x933.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104102/IMG_0809-scaled-e1687884162355.jpg)My new buff, to help me breathe when
outdoors

On the first day, I befriended a fellow thru-hiker, Skip (and his dog
Lizzie). Skip had a car and he drove me to the library (so I could post
for you, my lovely readers) and to the mall, where a group of us
gathered to water Guardians of the Galaxy (because what else is there to
do when the air quality outside is so horrendous that you cannot hang
out outdoors).

Because I ended up staying for 3 days, not only was I lucky enough to
be around for the hiker supper (where I ate probably the most food in
one sitting since I've been on the trail and it was DELICIOUS), but I
also got to go to the [Deer Head
Inn,](https://deerheadinn.com/) the oldest continuously running jazz club in the country.

[![](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104115/IMG_0812-700x525.jpg)](https://photos.thetrek.co/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/27104115/IMG_0812-scaled.jpg)Eating at the hiker potluck! Skip is to the
left of me
