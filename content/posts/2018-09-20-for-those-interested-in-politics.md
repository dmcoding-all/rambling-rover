The past couple of days I have had a couple
interesting conversations with some Scottish friends I have made and if
you are interested in the dynamics of Scottish Independence you may find
this post interesting. Mainly I am just going to recount a couple of the
facts my friends have told me that I find interesting:

- Currently, the Scottish government has the power to tax its people,
but not the power to enforce this taxation
- When taxes are collected, the taxes are brought down to West
Minister, where there is an algorithm which then decides how much money
each country in the UK receives back, and Scotland ends up receiving
less money back then they put in
- As I said in a previous post, if Scotland was to separate from the
UK, they would be eligible to join the Nordic Union ([Denmark](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denmark), [Finland](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finland), [Iceland](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iceland), [Norway](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norway) and [Sweden](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden)) as
well as their [autonomous
regions](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_regions) (the [Åland
Islands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%85land_Islands), the [Faroe
Islands](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faroe_Islands) and [Greenland](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland)).
- What I found extremely shocking was that my Scottish friend told me
that while in school (everything below university level) they were not
taught any Scottish history. He said (now this is definitely an opinion)
that he believes this was done on purpose, to not inspire any ideas of
independence in Scots
To summarize: my friend think Scottish independence is coming, and
probably sooner than later
If any of you are interested in this/have any questions feel free to ask
me! If I don't know I will ask around! Hope you found this
interesting
