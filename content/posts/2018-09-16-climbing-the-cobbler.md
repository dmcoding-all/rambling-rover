There is something about a Scottish mountain in September that feels completely different from anything I'd hiked before. Maybe it was the low clouds hanging over the Arrochar Alps, or the fact that I was doing it with a group of people I'd only met two weeks earlier. Whatever it was, the day we climbed Ben Arthur — better known locally as The Cobbler — is one I keep coming back to.

## Getting there

We took the ScotRail train from Edinburgh Waverley to Arrochar and Tarbet, which takes just under two hours and costs very little if you book in advance. The trailhead is a short walk from the station — you can see the mountain from the platform, which is both exciting and slightly intimidating.

## The hike

The route up is around 5 miles return with about 900 metres of ascent. It starts gradually through forest before opening out into open moorland. The summit — a distinctive rocky peak with a hole through it — requires a short scramble to reach. Getting through that hole to officially "top out" is a rite of passage, and completely optional if heights aren't your thing.

> The Cobbler doesn't look that tall from the train. Standing at the base of the summit rocks, you quickly recalibrate that assessment.

We had patches of cloud rolling through all day, which gave the whole thing a moody, dramatic quality that a bluebird day wouldn't have matched. By the time we reached the top, the clouds had cleared just enough for views over Loch Long and out toward the Firth of Clyde.

## What to bring

- Sturdy hiking boots (trail runners won't cut it on the summit rocks)
- Waterproofs — always, in Scotland, always
- More layers than you think you need
- A packed lunch; there's nothing at the trailhead

## Would I recommend it?

Unequivocally yes. It's one of the most accessible Munro-adjacent peaks near Edinburgh, and the summit scramble gives it a level of excitement that pure walking routes don't. It set the tone for the rest of my semester — I was in Scotland now, and I intended to use it.
