- Pre-Trail ResearchIn addition to reaching out to anyone and
everyone who I knew who had thru-hiked before, I studied up on what my
type of hike, a [Flip Flop](https://appalachiantrail.org/explore/hike-the-a-t/thru-hiking/flip-flop/), would look
like- Studied [how I would get food on the
hike](https://appalachiantrail.com/advice/planning-meals-for-a-thru-hike/) and what food I would eat[GearSkeptic: Hiker
Food](https://m.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLEu_UfyDKJALXcpeEtToxO9NEpwJKTKX_&fbclid=IwAR0PLAqGLh_e81NcmZuDa-sjcrGxB0nbwPL6R9g9aKf9_46ZvqqR9-p0WdI&mibextid=Zxz2cZ)- [Greenbelly: 76 backpacking
food ideas](https://www.greenbelly.co/pages/backpacking-food-ideas?fbclid=IwAR0Os9VVjMRwkhBBV8oREUqnPoKu-KFAVpzqo5WZcnKu8P87OcY-Zhbxqp0&mibextid=Zxz2cZ)

- Read up on [different health
issues](https://appalachiantrail.org/explore/plan-and-prepare/hiking-basics/health/) I may face while on the trail (i.e., ticks, poisonous
plants, snakes…)

- Studied [Leave No Trace](https://appalachiantrail.org/explore/plan-and-prepare/leave-no-trace/)

- Read up on different tips for Solo Female Thru-Hikers from websites
such as [AppalachianTrail.com](https://appalachiantrail.com/20170125/tips-for-solo-female-appalachian-trail-hikers/), [Hike&Cycle](https://hikeandcycle.com/37-tips-for-women-hiking-alone-bonus-female-hikers-blogs/), [SectionHiker](https://sectionhiker.com/10-appalachian-trail-tips-women/) which
led to me taking the following precautions that you can read about in my
last post [Precautions I took as
a solo-woman hiking the AT](https://theramblingrover.com/precautions-i-took-as-a-solo-woman-hiking-the-at/)

- I compiled a list of emergency contacts of friends and family
located on or near the AT so if anything went wrong on the trail I could
know if have someone nearby

- Gear/Shakedown Hikes

Shakedown hikes turned out to be one of the most valuable things I
did in order to prep for my hike (see my posts on my [1st](https://theramblingrover.com/what-i-learned-from-my-5-day-shakedown-hike-in-patagonia/), [2nd](https://theramblingrover.com/joshua-tree-national-park-backpacking-shakedown-2/), and [3rd](https://theramblingrover.com/secret-canyon-trail-backpacking-shakedown-3/) shakedown hike). A lot of gear sounds nice in
theory, but in reality can be a pain in the butt!

- Learned to [spray my tent and clothes with
Permethrin](https://sectionhiker.com/treating-your-clothes-with-permethrin/) for bug protection (with help from my supportive
mother)

- ![](https://dcabroad.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/d1e09-img_0054-1-scaled-1.jpeg)

- Modified my gear, such as my...

water bladder so that I can fill it up without removing it from my
backpack using a [sawyer
attachment kit](https://www.rei.com/product/858765/sawyer-fast-fill-hydration-pack-adapter-kit) (I recommend just making sure this kit fits your
bladder or you may end up like some of the angry reviewers who cut their
hose only to have the kit not fit).

- Pot! I created a pot cozy from a car sun shade using guidance from
[AntiGravityGear](https://antigravitygear.com/shop/product-category/antigravitygear-pot-cozy-kit/)

- Gathered some First Aid/Emergency essentials, including but not
limited to:

First Aid: [zinc-oxide](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00ZQXT4EY?psc=1&ref=ppx_yo2ov_dt_b_product_details) for butt
chafing, a menstrual cup (I began using one in 2018 and I have never
looked back), a [Kulu pee rag](https://www.rei.com/product/182956/kula-cloth-reusable-antimicrobial-pee-cloth) and
a [CuloClean ](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07L448T4K?psc=1&smid=A1V0PQWRKGJ9P3&ref_=chk_typ_imgToDp)trail
bidet, anti-itch cream for bug bites and poisonous plant rashes, [tick
tweezers](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00KI1I7BU?psc=1&ref=ppx_yo2ov_dt_b_product_details), a small bag of corn-starch for jock itch, basic over the
counter drugs such as Motrin, Benadryl , and an EpiPen for a worst
case scenario

- Emergency: I created for myself a “mini sewing kit” which really was
just a needle+long thread, duck tape that I wrapped around my hiking
poles

- Online tools

[Appalachian Trail Database](http://www.sophiaknows.com/atdb/index.html) 

- [PostHoler](https://www.postholer.com/planner/hikePlanner.php) A website
with many helpful tools, howeverI mainly used it to create a rough
estimate of my timeline on the trail. I copied and pasted this into an
google sheet that I shared with family and friends so they know where I
should be as time passes

- Created a [LighterPack](https://lighterpack.com/welcome) to know how
much my pack would weigh.

- Websites worth bookmarking (for use both before and on the
trail)

[Appalachian Trail Weather](https://www.atweather.org/)

Appalachian Trail](https://www.whiteblaze.net/forum/content.php/220-Post-Offices-along-the-Appalachian-Trail)

- Made sure my Garmin Account/GPS was readyI tested out my
Garmin during my shakedown hikes to ensure I knew how to use
it.- Set up my emergency contacts.- Upgraded my Garmin
subscription to [the Recreation
plan](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/837461/pn/010-06001-SU) that way I have 40+ texts/month and unlimited location
requests.

- Download GPX files of the trail to upload to my Garmin.

This was the [site](http://guymott.com/atgps.html) where I
downloaded the highest resolution GPX files (39 files)

- Here is [website link](http://topofusion.com/at-gps.php) for GPX
files that break down the trail into 19 sections (lower resolution than
the link above but better if you’re tight on storage on your GPS)

- And I gave my friends/family the ability to track my location.
