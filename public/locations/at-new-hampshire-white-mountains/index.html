AT — New Hampshire (White Mountains)

Aug 2023

The White Mountains are where the Appalachian Trail stops being hard and starts being genuinely serious. The terrain above treeline in New Hampshire is exposed, rocky, and at the mercy of weather systems that form faster than almost anywhere else in the eastern United States. I arrived in New Hampshire after 1,800 miles of trail and found muscles I didn’t know I had being recruited for granite scrambles that would be technical hikes in any other state. It is also, without question, the most beautiful section of the entire trail. The Franconia Ridge on a clear August morning — exposed ridgeline, 360-degree views, the full arc of the White Mountains laid out in every direction — is an image I go back to often.

Hike
Franconia Ridge Loop
One of the most spectacular ridge walks in the northeast — over 3 miles of exposed ridgeline connecting Little Haystack, Lincoln, and Lafayette. Do it on a clear day only. Scrambled it during my thru-hike in perfect conditions.
Hike
Presidential Range Traverse
The full traverse from Madison to Washington covers multiple 5,000ft+ summits. The weather on Mt. Washington is genuinely unpredictable and dangerous — treat it with respect. The Cog Railway is a fun way down if your legs are cooked.
Stay
AMC Huts (Lakes of the Clouds, etc.)
The AMC operates a series of mountain huts along the AT through the Whites. Expensive but extraordinary — hot meals, bunks, and views you cannot otherwise access. Thru-hikers can sometimes work for stay. Book months ahead for summer dates.

Stay
Gorham, NH (town stop)
The main resupply town for thru-hikers doing the Whites. The Libby House Inn is beloved by the hiking community. Everything you need in a small package.
Tip
The Whites will take longer than you expect
If you're section hiking, the terrain is significantly harder than Pennsylvania or Virginia. Budget 50–70% more time per mile than your usual pace. The reward is proportional.
Tip
Check Mt. Washington Observatory forecasts
The weather on the Presidential Range can go from fine to genuinely life-threatening within hours. Check mountwashington.org the morning of any high-altitude hiking.
