Europe · Scotland

Edinburgh, Scotland

Sep 2018 – Dec 2018

Edinburgh was my home for three months during my semester abroad at the University of Edinburgh — and it remains one of my favourite cities in the world. It’s walkable, dramatically beautiful, and packed with history around every corner. The Old Town and New Town feel like two completely different cities stacked on top of each other. I arrived in September knowing almost no one and left in December with some of the best friends I’ve made travelling. If you get the chance to spend real time here — not just a weekend — take it.

Do
Arthur's Seat
Hike to the top of this ancient volcano right in the city for panoramic views over Edinburgh. Go at sunrise — you'll likely have it to yourself.
Do
Royal Mile & Old Town
Walk the full length of the Royal Mile from Edinburgh Castle to Holyrood Palace. Explore the closes (alleyways) branching off either side.
Do
Calton Hill
Easy 10-minute walk up for great views of the city skyline, the Firth of Forth, and Arthur's Seat. Free, and gorgeous at golden hour.
Hike
The Cobbler (Ben Arthur)
A fantastic day hike about an hour from Edinburgh near Arrochar. The rocky summit scramble is genuinely exciting. Solid boots required.
Hike
Isle of Skye Day Trip
Worth the 3-hour drive from Edinburgh. Hire a car and do the Fairy Pools and Old Man of Storr in one day — or better yet, stay overnight.
Eat
The Outsider
Fantastic views of Edinburgh Castle from the window. Great food at a fair price — book ahead, it fills up fast.
Eat
Mosque Kitchen
An Edinburgh institution. Enormous portions of curry served outdoors on paper plates. Cheap, delicious, and always busy.
Stay
Castle Rock Hostel
Right next to the castle, social common areas, and great for meeting other travellers. Classic Edinburgh hostel experience.
Tip
Get a Ridacard (bus pass)
If you're staying more than a week, the Ridacard pays for itself quickly. Lothian Buses cover the whole city and are very reliable.
